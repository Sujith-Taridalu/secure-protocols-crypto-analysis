# Certificate and IPSec Analysis

## Certificate Analysis

The source work covered certificate validation, issuer trust, chain hierarchy, and expiration checks. This is useful because it shows an ability to move past “the browser says secure” and explain why trust is established.

Important themes included:

- certificate authority hierarchy
- issuer reputation and browser trust
- certificate lifecycle awareness
- practical PKI interpretation

## IPSec Analysis

The IPSec portion focused on replay protection through sequence numbers and sliding windows. This is a good example of translating protocol mechanics into security outcomes.

Important themes included:

- anti-replay protection
- integrity as a companion to sequence tracking
- the role of authentication in secure packet handling
- why sequence numbers matter in real deployments

## Why These Topics Matter

Together, these examples show that protocol security is not just about memorizing acronyms. It is about understanding how trust, integrity, and secure communication are actually enforced.
